# Leader safety briefing

> Draft requiring review by qualified safeguarding, legal, insurance and medical professionals before operational adoption.

## Purpose

Draft leader safety briefing template using synthetic fields only.

## Intended Outcome

A trained local team can use this document to make consistent preparations, run the work safely, and preserve programme continuity.

## Accountable Owner

Journey Lead, with the named functional lead accountable for the specific workstream.

## Supporting Roles

Oversight Lead, Administration Lead, Logistics Lead, Safety Lead, Finance Lead, Communications Lead and Session Coordinator as applicable.

## Timing

Use this guidance during launch planning, weekly preparation, live delivery and post-activity review.

## Inputs

Current source register, approved templates, local venue information, leader availability and human-reviewed safety requirements.

## Dependencies

Role clarity, documented decisions, privacy-safe records and review of any legal, safeguarding, medical, insurance or theological assumptions.

## Preparation Timeline

- Confirm owner and reviewers.
- Adapt local details without changing core content.
- Prepare materials, communications and safety controls.
- Complete readiness review before delivery.

## Step-by-step Process

- Review this document and related templates.
- Confirm local context and constraints.
- Assign accountable owners and due dates.
- Run the activity using the checklist.
- Record evidence, issues and improvement actions.

## Outputs

Completed checklist, updated tracker, saved evidence of completion and post-activity learning.

## Risks

Privacy exposure, unclear ownership, incomplete safety review, over-localising canonical material and relying on undocumented assumptions.

## Controls

Use redacted examples, approval checkpoints, source references, human-review labels and validation scripts.

## Communications

Use approved communication templates and avoid sharing sensitive information in open groups.

## Evidence of Completion

Signed-off readiness checklist, completed register entries, attendance where appropriate and post-activity review notes.

## Post-activity Review

Capture what worked, what failed, incidents, follow-up actions and updates needed before the next Journey.

## Permitted Adaptations

Adapt dates, venues, suppliers, local laws, language and staffing while preserving programme intent and safety controls.
