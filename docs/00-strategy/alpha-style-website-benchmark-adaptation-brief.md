## Benchmark Observations

The public Alpha pages separate discovery from host operations. Guest-facing pages guide people toward trying Alpha, while leader-facing pages route hosts into a registered operating area for resources, training, schedule planning, promotion, team access and guest support.

Observed reusable patterns:

- Separate public pathways for people who want to try the programme and leaders who want to run it.
- A post-registration host area for course setup, resources, schedule, promotion, team access and guest support.
- A run pathway that brings registration, training, invitation and support together instead of leaving leaders to search a folder archive.
- Resource grouping around series materials, schedule, promotion, team and guests.
- Context routes for audiences or settings such as youth, churches, schools, online, homes, workplaces and prisons.
- A simple session experience model based on connection, watched content and conversation.
- A digital resource centre model for videos, guides, briefing material, training, promotional assets, scheduling and team resources.
- Clear separation between public invitation material and private leader/team resources.

## Journey Adaptation Principles

- Use the benchmark as an operating-pattern reference only.
- Create original Journey wording, visuals, videos and templates.
- Keep Drive and live Sheets as the source of truth until production authority approves otherwise.
- Separate public explanation from protected leader resources and restricted operational data.
- Make leader assignments, responsibilities, schedules, review gates and publication readiness visible in one hub.
- Show review status and approval dependencies anywhere draft or restricted material appears.
- Treat guest, participant, medical, safeguarding, finance, contact and private leader data as restricted unless explicit approval says otherwise.

## Recommended Journey Website Layers

1. Public discovery layer: what The Journey is, who it serves, the approved invitation path and approved public dates only.
2. Start a Journey layer: readiness pathway, minimum team, leadership commitments and how an approved team starts.
3. Leader operating layer: dashboard, role assignments, tasks, events, decisions and review gates.
4. Resource library layer: approved guides, templates, session packs, event packs and Drive links with status labels.
5. Content approval layer: Website Content Review rows, approval templates and publication-handoff evidence.
6. Protected leader layer: training, facilitation notes, event logistics, team resources and role-specific resources.
7. Restricted data layer: participant administration, medical, safeguarding, finance and private operational records kept out of public pages.

## Required Pre-Publication Evidence

- Public explanation and Start a Journey copy reviewed through Website Content Review and the applicable human-review rows.
- Videos, media, testimonials, screenshots and third-party references reviewed through HR-007 before any public or protected use.
- Dates and event rhythm approved by the logistics owner with a change owner recorded.
- Leader names and role assignments approved through owner appointment routing and governance authority review.
- Restricted-data placement approved before participant, medical, safeguarding, finance or private operational workflows are connected to a website.
- DEC-004 records the access model before any production website build proceeds.
- DEC-006 and TASK-016 record approved version, production URL, rollback owner and publication authority before publication.

## No-Copy Boundary

Do not copy Alpha assets, page copy, media, screenshots, course structure, branded design elements or proprietary resources into Journey V3. Build the Journey site from Journey-owned content, local review evidence and approved source-of-truth Drive materials. If Alpha material is referenced, use a link or high-level attribution only when copyright approval permits it.

## Prototype Impact

The current operating hub prototype may continue to model these patterns synthetically: Dashboard, START HERE, Planning, Templates, Gate Review, Access, Content and Drive Copy. It remains a review artifact, not a production deployment.
